Boids Implementation in Javascript

Status: in progress • Date: 2026-01-21 • Tags: javascript, boids, algorithms

The Boids algorithm was developed in 1986 by Craig Reynolds in order to mimic the flocking and flying behaviour of birds. The name "Boid" refers to a bird-oid object, an object that can move in a way similar to that of a flock of birds.

$ summary

The boids algorithm itself only relies on three main concepts. The first concept is seperation, the orienting of a boid away from those that are in the vicinity. The second is alignment, or heading in the average direction of those in the vicinity. The last concept is cohesion, or the preference to be at the center of mass of those in the vicinity.

The wikipedia article on the boids algorithm goes into greater detail, and has a great visual aid for the three concepts.

The combined effect of seperation, alignment, and cohesion results in emergent behaviour that looks surprisingly like a flock of birds.
